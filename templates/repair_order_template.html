What is a repair order?

A repair order is the intake and work-authorization document used when a vehicle first arrives at the shop. It records the customer complaint, requested service, vehicle details, and technician or advisor notes before final pricing or billing is completed.

What should an auto repair work order include?

A professional repair order should include the shop name and contact information, the customer’s name, phone, and address, the vehicle year, make, model, VIN, mileage, the customer concern, requested service, technician notes, and a clear authorization section.

Repair order vs estimate vs invoice

A repair order is the intake and work-authorization document. An estimate shows the projected cost before work is approved. An invoice is the final bill after the repair is complete and actual charges are known.
