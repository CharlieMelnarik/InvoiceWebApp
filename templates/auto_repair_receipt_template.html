What is an auto repair receipt?

An auto repair receipt is the proof-of-payment document a shop gives the customer after payment has already been received. It confirms the amount paid, payment date, payment method, and often serves as the customer’s service and warranty record.

What should a repair receipt include?

A professional repair receipt should include the shop name and contact information, customer information, vehicle details, receipt number, payment date, payment method, amount paid, any related invoice number, remaining balance if one exists, and a short work summary.

Receipt vs invoice: what is the difference?

An invoice is the bill showing what is owed. A receipt is the document showing that payment was received. A receipt should clearly confirm the payment date, amount paid, and payment method, while an invoice focuses on the amount due.
