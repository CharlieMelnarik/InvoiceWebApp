What is an auto repair estimate?

An auto repair estimate is the projected cost of proposed repair work before the final job is completed. It helps the shop explain the expected labor, parts, taxes, and service notes so the customer understands the likely cost before authorizing the work.

What should an auto repair estimate include?

A professional repair estimate should include the business name and contact information, the customer’s name and address, the vehicle year, make, model, VIN, projected labor and parts line items, taxes, the estimated total, and notes or disclaimers about how the final bill may change after inspection.

Estimate vs invoice: what is the difference?

An estimate shows the projected cost of proposed repairs before the work is completed. An invoice is the final bill after the work is done and actual labor, parts, taxes, and any additional approved repairs are known.
